About Me

I'm a PhD student interested in the rhetoric of memory, everday objects, and multimodal scholarship.

I have an undergrade and two masters degrees from Xavier University, and am now pursuing a PhD in Rhetoric and Composition at the University of Cincinnati. My current research explores storytelling, memory, and relationships through personal objects and everyday “stuff.” I am especially interested in projects that integrate participatory art, collage, and mixed media into academic research and writing.

I have nearly a decade of teaching experience and am interested in a university teaching position after earning my PhD. I have taught a variety of courses for both high school and college undergraduates in many areas of English and writing studies, including literature, journalism, composition, and rhetoric.

Outside of school, I'm a marathon runner, cross country coach, housplant hoarder, and dog mom to two retired greyhounds.

Current Projects

My current projects represent a range of my research interested, including the study of memory, everyday objects, and multimodal scholarship. These projects were created for courses in my PhD program and are currently undergoing revision for future publication.

Autopsy of a Breakup: The Matter of Love and Loss

This multimedia project explores the way people process grief and loss through material objects, using an archival study of over 200 items donated to the Museum of Broken Relationships in Zagreb, Croatia.

Read the Essay

A Lifetime of Writing (So Far)

This collage-style literacy narrative explores my personal history with writing, while exploring broader questions about how and why we write, and the ways these processes and motivations change over the course of a lifetime.

Read the Essay

(Re)Composition: The Life and Afterlife of Everyday Objects

This web text explores the storytelling power of everyday objects through (and beyond) the lifespan. This project explores my grandmother and the objects she kept throughout her life, along with the stories of the family members who have kept these everyday objects after her death.
